Livecoding

As Staxl I'm engaging myself with live coding languages and tools for audio and video. My entry point for this was always SuperCollider and TidalCycles. However, there's quite a bit of logic you can do in Ableton and with Max4Live, too. So I'm also using that for algorithmic live stuff. Furthermore, I'm utilizing a monome norns in live situations sometimes. My approach is rather experimental and basically inspired by the tools I use and by the ideas that float around in the SuperCollider/TidalCycles and the monome community - and also by breakcore music quite a bit.

I haven't done a hell lot of livecoding performances myself yet, but here's some snippets:

TOPLAP Leipzig

TOPLAP stands for Temporal Organisation for the Pragmatics of Live Artistic Programming and is an interntional network of coders, artists, organizers and all kinds of people that are into creative and artistic approaches to programing, hacking and coding. (You can read all about in the TOPLAP Wiki.) Despite being a decentralized group, they got nodes (i.e. local organizations) in various cities around the globe. However, Leipzig, where I'm based, is missing on the map, which is why I'm trying to get TOPLAP Leipzig going in the near future. I love to get in touch with people who are interested in livecoding, algorithmic music, open-source music tools etc. and to share skills and ideas. If you're interested in an exchange, or if you want to participate, hit me up!

Practice

My musical practice is a lot about livecoding and music production with open-source software tools.

While it is very common still among music producers, beat makers and so on to use corporate and oftentimes expensive software to make music at home on the computer (or in the studio), there's also a plethora of open-source software tools that allow users to do music with a minimal (or zero) budget and get results that are just as exciting and inspiring as what you'd get by using popular applications. One of the great things about engaging one with these open-source tools is that you will quickly be introduced to a vast community of people that work on and with these applications and are ready to share their knowledge and expand the possibilities of these apps.

Some tools I really dig are ORCΛ, ppooll and Cardinal.

I use these tools in order to record and arrange musical ideas with and algorithmically generate MIDI notes and sequences, create and design sounds and present musical ideas on stage (or in a stream).

Since I'm relying a lot on open-source tools and the communities around it, I don't try to get commercial with this. ;)
