Livecoding

I started engaging myself with coding languages for audio and video, and with livecoding languages in particular in 2020 when COVID-19 hit by getting into SuperCollider and related things. My approach is rather experimental and basically inspired by the tools I use and by all the ideas that float around in the livecoding community - and also by breakcore music a bit, hehe.

I haven't done a hell lot of livecoding performances myself yet, but here's some recent snippets:

TOPLAP Leipzig

TOPLAP stands for Temporal Organisation for the Pragmatics of Live Artistic Programming and is an interntional network of coders, artists, organizers and all kinds of people that are into creative and artistic approaches to programing, hacking and coding. (You can read all about in the TOPLAP Wiki.) Despite being a decentralized group, they got nodes (i.e. local organizations) in various cities around the globe. However, Leipzig, where I'm based, is missing on the map, which is why I'm trying to get TOPLAP Leipzig going in the near future. I love to get in touch with people who are interested in livecoding, algorithmic music, open-source music tools etc. and to share skills and ideas. If you're interested in an exchange, or if you want to participate, hit me up!

Workshop

I also started to offer workshops on livecoding and music production with free software tools.

While it is very common still among music producers, beat makers and so on to use corporate and oftentimes expensive software to make music at home on the computer (or in the studio), there's also a plethora of free and open-source software tools that allow users to do music with a minimal (or zero) budget and get results that are just as exciting and inspiring as what you'd get by using popular applications like Ableton Live, Serum etc. One of the great things about engaging one with these open-source tools is that you will quickly be introduced to a vast community of people that work on and with these applications and are ready to share their knowledge and expand the possibilities of these apps.

In my workshops, I present some of these software tools and share ideas on how to work and perform with free music-making software. The workshop is mostly for beginners, but even those that are already familiar with making music on the computer will probably get some new insights into the world of open-source music apps and learn about new and exciting tools like ORCÎ›, TidalCycles, VCV Rack and others.

During the course of the workshop, I will give an introduction on how to record and arrange musical ideas with these software tools and people will learn how to algorithmically generate MIDI notes and sequences, be introduced to ways of creating and designing sounds and hear about livecoding as one approach to presenting your musical ideas on stage or in a live stream.

Since I'm relying a lot on open-source tools and the communities around it, I don't do these workshops commercially. ;)
